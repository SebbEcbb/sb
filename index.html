Piano improvisation #316

The story of A man

True story, from the heart
A descendant of Reuben,
first born of Jacob & his cousin

On a cold winterday in January of 1971, late at night, after 24 hours of tough labor, a boy was born in France, near Saint-Etienne.

French father, courageous Dutch mother.

Unfortunately, they divorced a year after. Cultural miscomprehension. The boy grew up with his mother in the Netherlands. Living in various places, he moved about 12 times in 25 years. That tendency would only increase over the next 25 years.



Foundation: The Netherlands

A happy boy, somewhat tender and gentle, certainly full of energy. Good at maths, sports and music. As a child, he never learned to work hard. Football was nicer. And he was good too. As child he played with Martin Jol. Still proud. But mummy moved on, so he lost contact. He started piano at 11. Mum wanted him to. Childhood was pretty tough, as stranger in the grey lowlands.

Helped by his girlfriend and her loving parents, he got his high school degree. A miracle, seen the circumstances. After working in a bank for one year, he managed to enter the High School for Arts in Utrecht. His mother bought him a piano, with exceptional sound.

He studied classical piano music for four years. Gifted, but hard for him to focus on anything. He had relational problems and was interested in too many other things. He also spent a lot of time trying to make his own music, in need of finding his identity.

Occupied with philosophical questions, he read a lot. Stoned, most of the time. Books like the Torah, the I-Tjing, Freud and Carl Jung became very interesting, under influence of good Dutch marihuana. Lol.

In such a state, understanding absolutely nothing of what he read, he spent a lot of time on meditation and writing. Or spacing out, if you prefer. The rest of his time, he improvised on his fine piano.

Well, he did argue weekly, for two years, with an elder of the Jehovah's witnesses. About God and the reasons of life. To provoke and test the poor old man, who said he knew God. But also out of curiosity.

He'd had some very extra-ordinary experiences. It'd stirred up great curiosity and started a spiritual path. He was sure he had to discover hidden things.

But going to work? Nah,...not for him. He was thinking!

Entire days he would try to compose music, sometimes for 15 hours. But he could not get devoted to practising his parts for Art School. His mind was too busy.

His complicated lifestyle made any social life very difficult. Relationships broke, for lack of stability and maturity. His creative mind went all over the place, and it was hard for others to bear up with him.

After several failing love affairs, he found himself alone again at 24, living on a boat in the centre of Amsterdam. The rent was 3 times his monthly budget, available as a student. With his piano, he had found no better solution than to live on a boat. He did play for about 15 hours a day. Income : 450 p/m, rent 1100 p/m. What the heck kept him going?

All this generated stress. The broken relationships, studies that had suffered lack of concentration, lack of money, daily smoking marihuana, and other stuff, like being around the wrong people. His mental state made it even harder to concentrate on anything.

He did one good thing though: improvise at the Amsterdam Marriott Hotel, on business meals and conferences. He always improvised. They liked it and paid him well. But the occasions were too little. He struggled to stop smoking and get life going.

He went through some kind of identity crisis. Poor, needy and unable to integrate in any structure. Even to the point that he felt Holland was not his home land anymore. Then he got an idea: "leave Holland,try France." He left for good. At 25, a first life ended.

Second chance: France

His birth country.
A new start?

Living with his father, he tried to be accepted at the National Superior Conservatory in Lyon for a composer's study. Bad idea. He had worked hard for 6 months and produced 6 pieces of music. He was satisfied with his work.

The director had politely invited him to tell him there was no place for him. The 3 or 4 spots were already taken. He wondered if the man had even looked at his work.

He was disappointed. But at least he'd finished something. It had inspired him. Music friends liked his piano pieces.

Just like with anyone, the relationship with his father was difficult. They were friends, but both strong heads. Not very suitable to live together. He had to go anyway… find his life. He left his father after the unpleasant school experience and went to visit a friend in Bordeaux.

From there he met a community. Believers. People, living on a farm in the South of France. Back then, that was still possible.

Their life was impressive to him. Lots of people. Hard workers. He liked it. There was a certain peace : something he had been missing in his life. He stayd 3 months and many things happened. He ended up being a believer himself and returned a year later. He wanted to know God and love. And he wanted work hard.

Farm-life had a lot going on: Business and many kinds of projects. They worked hard, 6 days a week, with friends, often for 20 hours a day. He enjoyed being with people and learning to work. Farming, gardening, baking, construction, mechanics, wood works, animals, and more. He also learned a lot about social matters. Relationships, communication, collectivity, team-work, and more.

After a few years, he started to do the bookkeeping, on request of others. There was a need in that domain. He loved it and end up doing it for over 10 years. He learned, working closely with the professional accountants. He also learned some programming skills and web development. At social events he played piano with other musicians.

In the strength of his life, with love and zeal for his newfound occupations, he had become a pretty zealous and motivated person. Ready to do a lot for the people he loved. He had finally found a reason to be wholehearted and have self-respect.

After eight years, he got married. They raised four children. Not without trouble, but usually they were happy.

10 years of married life passed, relatively peaceful. Compared to what was about to come. Some serious problems arose, especially for his family.

As everything works out for the good, to those who don't resist the flow of life, as painful as it can be, the years following exposed some deeply disappointing facts. And everything changed again. For good.

A third life started .... again with music.

TO BE CONTINUED.....maybe!
