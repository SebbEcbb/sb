A man must live from the heart

Who am I ?

Born in France, near Saint-Etienne, 1971. French dad, Dutch mum.
I grew up in Holland, from age 1. Good in math, sports and music. Started piano at 11. Mum was determined.

Education

After high school, I worked as cashier in a bank.

Did pretty well, but artistic aspirations urged. I believed I would regret not trying School for Higher Arts & Music. With a year of preparation, I was accepted. Studied 4 years with Alwin Bär.

Then concluded that music was to express and not to live. I also wanted to discover my roots and birth country. So I left for France.

With new friends I learned to work hard. Regularly played music with others, but only for pleasure.

Entering into Finance

The company had bookkeeping needs. Drawn to the job, as descendant of an accountant of the Dutch Ministry of Finance, I probably inherited my grandfather's gift with numbers.

Co-owner of the business, I volunteered and I ended up financial manager for two enterprises.

The mission:
"Create order in finances and produce the legal statements."

Supervised and trained by accountants, I learned the bookkeeping skills. After 3 years of learning, I did the work without them.

I developed applications, under Excel & VBA, to automate the tasks. It allowed any user to enter transactions and transformed them into importable journals. Those were imported with a click into the software.

Entrepreneur

After 14 years, the company changed policy and direction. It was time to look for new experiences. I started a business in commerce, selling products I knew from the past.

Worked well, till COVID19 ruined my markets. Impossible to make a living for a family with 4 kids. So I switched again. Took on jobs for customers. My business was now a multi-task construction and maintenance company.

Web Developer & Content-creator

Over the years, at night, I invested many hours in learning code. By need and for pleasure. I discovered an excellent way for creativity and artistic expression.

Passionate about this technology, making it possible to create applications, tools & content, and serve illimited numbers of people all around the world, I consider the possibilities endless.

Piano & YouTube

So what happened to the piano ?

There is a time for everything. After hard work, it is time to relax. Artistic expression is essential for life, but it has its time and place.

I believe art must mature and ripen, just like fruit. No body picks a ripe apple from a recently planted tree. A tree must grow and the apple must ripen. Unripe fruit is not enjoyable. Art needs patience. An artist needs to grow into what he has to offer. For me, art has to become a supernatural gift. The process is organic and in my opinion cannot be accelerated. It is given by nature.

More thoughts on artists and what their work is.

I play for artistic expression, not to make a living. I share it for the same reason. I do not expect people to love it. It doesn't matter. I value all the more those who do, since it proves we have something in common.

What counts is to advance in what we love. In my case, the creation of artistic expression, with words and music. Or with any work. If we do what we love, it will naturally grow into perfection. Just like life itself.

So whatever your heart says, if it doesn"t hurt others, go for it!
